Getting Started

What is MassaPay?
MassaPay is a self-custodial mobile wallet for the Massa blockchain. It allows you to securely store, send, receive, and swap MAS tokens and other assets on the Massa network. Your private keys are stored locally on your device and never shared with anyone.
How do I create a new wallet?
  1. Download MassaPay from GitHub releases
  2. Open the app and tap "Create New Wallet"
  3. Write down your 24-word recovery phrase on paper (never screenshot it!)
  4. Verify the phrase by selecting words in order
  5. Set a PIN or enable biometric authentication
Your wallet is now ready to use!
Can I import my Massa Station wallet?
Yes! MassaPay is fully compatible with Massa Station and Bearby wallets. You can import your existing wallet using your 24-word recovery phrase. The derivation path m/44'/632'/0'/0' is the same across all Massa wallets.
Can I use the same wallet on multiple devices?
Yes, you can import your wallet on multiple devices using your 24-word recovery phrase. However, for security reasons, we recommend keeping your primary wallet on a single device. Remember: anyone with your recovery phrase has full access to your funds.

Security

Is MassaPay safe to use?
Yes, MassaPay is designed with security as a top priority:
  • 100% Open Source - You can verify every line of code on GitHub
  • Industry-standard cryptography - secp256k1 curve, AES-256 encryption
  • Local storage only - Keys never leave your device
  • Android Keystore - Hardware-backed security when available
  • VirusTotal verified - 0/71 detections
  • Zero data collection - No analytics, no tracking
Is my recovery phrase stored securely?
Your recovery phrase is encrypted using AES-256 and stored in Android's secure enclave (Keystore). It never leaves your device and is never transmitted over the network. The encryption key is protected by your PIN/biometrics and cannot be extracted even if your device is rooted.
What happens if I lose my phone?
As long as you have your 24-word recovery phrase written down safely, you can restore your wallet on any new device. Your funds are stored on the Massa blockchain, not on your phone. Without the recovery phrase, there is no way to recover your wallet - so always keep it safe offline!
Does MassaPay collect any data?
No. MassaPay collects absolutely zero user data. There are no analytics, no crash reporting services, no telemetry. The app communicates only with Massa blockchain nodes to fetch balances and broadcast transactions. Your privacy is fully respected.

Technical Details

What's the derivation path used?
MassaPay uses the standard Massa derivation path: m/44'/632'/0'/0'
  • 44' - BIP-44 purpose (hardened)
  • 632' - Massa coin type (hardened)
  • 0' - Account index (hardened)
  • 0' - Change index (hardened)
This ensures compatibility with Massa Station, Bearby, and any other standard Massa wallet.
What elliptic curve does Massa use?
Massa uses the secp256k1 elliptic curve - the same battle-tested curve used by Bitcoin and Ethereum. Key features:
  • 256-bit security level
  • ECDSA for digital signatures
  • Keccak-256 hashing for address derivation
  • Base58Check encoding for addresses
What RPC nodes does MassaPay connect to?
By default, MassaPay connects to official Massa nodes:
  • Mainnet: https://mainnet.massa.net/api/v2
  • Buildnet: https://buildnet.massa.net/api/v2
You can configure custom RPC endpoints in Settings if you run your own node or prefer a different provider.
Is MassaPay compatible with hardware wallets?
Currently, no hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor) has native Massa support. MassaPay cannot connect to hardware wallets until Massa develops an official hardware wallet app. However, MassaPay's local security with Android Keystore provides strong protection for your keys.

Features

What tokens are supported?
MassaPay supports:
  • MAS - Native Massa token
  • MRC-20 tokens - All fungible tokens on Massa (WETH, USDC, DAI, etc.)
  • MRC-721 NFTs - Non-fungible tokens and collectibles
You can add custom tokens by entering the smart contract address.
How does staking work?
Massa uses a "roll" system for staking:
  1. Buy rolls - Each roll costs 100 MAS
  2. Stake automatically - Your rolls participate in block production
  3. Earn rewards - Receive MAS for validating blocks
  4. Sell rolls - Convert back to liquid MAS anytime
MassaPay shows estimated APY and allows you to manage rolls directly from the app.
How do token swaps work?
Everything happens inside MassaPay - no need to visit external websites!

We use the Dusa protocol (Massa's main DEX) under the hood to execute swaps:
  • Select tokens to swap (e.g., MAS → USDC)
  • Enter amount and see real-time quote
  • Review price impact and fees
  • Confirm and sign the transaction
MassaPay acts as an all-in-one solution: you get the same DEX functionality without leaving your wallet. Swaps are executed on-chain through Dusa's liquidity pools, but the entire experience is integrated into our app.
Why can't I see my NFTs?
MassaPay scans known NFT collections automatically. If your NFT doesn't appear:
  1. Go to NFT Gallery
  2. Tap "Add Collection"
  3. Enter the smart contract address
  4. The app will scan and discover your NFTs
New collections may take a moment to index.

Troubleshooting

What if MassaPay stops working or gets removed?
Your funds are always safe! MassaPay is just a window to the blockchain - your crypto lives on Massa, not in the app.

If MassaPay ever stops working, gets uninstalled, or becomes unavailable:
  1. Your 24-word recovery phrase is universal - It works with any compatible wallet
  2. Import to Bearby - Browser extension wallet for Massa
  3. Import to Massa Station - Official desktop wallet
  4. Any BIP-39 compatible Massa wallet - Same derivation path works everywhere

We use the standard derivation path m/44'/632'/0'/0' - the same as Bearby and Massa Station. Your seed phrase will generate the exact same address in any of these wallets.

This is the power of self-custody: You're never locked into one app. Your keys, your crypto, your freedom.
Why does the dApps browser use Bearby instead of MassaPay?
When you connect to dApps through MassaPay's built-in browser, the connection uses the Bearby protocol. This is because most dApp frontends (like Dusa, NFT marketplaces, etc.) only have Bearby integration available as their wallet connection option - they haven't added MassaPay as a native choice yet.

Don't worry - it's still your wallet! MassaPay injects Bearby-compatible signatures so you can use any Massa dApp seamlessly. Your transactions are signed with your MassaPay private keys, not Bearby's. As more dApps add native MassaPay support, we'll update the integration.
Why is my balance showing 0?
If your balance shows 0 when it shouldn't:
  • Check network - Make sure you're on the correct network (Mainnet vs Buildnet)
  • Pull to refresh - Swipe down to force a balance update
  • Check RPC status - The node might be temporarily unavailable
  • Verify address - Ensure you imported the correct wallet
Transaction is stuck/pending
Massa has fast block times (~16 seconds), so transactions usually confirm quickly. If a transaction is stuck:
  • Wait a minute - Network congestion can cause delays
  • Check explorer - View your address on Massa Explorer
  • Insufficient fees - The transaction might have failed
Unlike Ethereum, Massa doesn't have a mempool, so stuck transactions will either confirm or fail.
App crashes or freezes
If MassaPay crashes:
  1. Force close the app and reopen
  2. Check for updates on GitHub releases
  3. Clear app cache (Settings → Apps → MassaPay → Clear Cache)
  4. If issues persist, report on GitHub with device info
Your funds are always safe - they're stored on the blockchain, not in the app.
How do I report a bug?
Found a bug? Help us improve MassaPay:
  1. Go to GitHub Issues
  2. Click "New Issue"
  3. Describe the bug with steps to reproduce
  4. Include device model and Android version
Never share your recovery phrase when reporting issues!
